Research

The "Not Stoic" Project

Status: Informal Research · Ongoing

I am currently investigating the modern misapplication of the terms "Stoicism" and "stoic." In recent years, these concepts have been heavily commodified and misunderstood in popular culture.

My core hypothesis is that this trend stems from an innate human need to appear polished, resilient, and unbothered on the outside, without actually making the difficult, underlying changes to one's character, virtues, and personality.

Key Areas of Exploration:

  • Aesthetic vs. Philosophy: How Stoicism became a superficial "life hack" rather than a rigorous framework for living.
  • Emotional Suppression: The dangerous conflation of merely hiding your feelings with actual emotional regulation.
  • The Polish vs. The Core: Why it is easier for people to adopt the appearance of a Stoic than to do the internal work required to build a solid character.
